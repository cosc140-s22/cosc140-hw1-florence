Brief History of Wales

Timeline

  • Stone Age: Celtic tribes lived as hunter gatherers
  • ~ 50 AD- 400 AD: The Romans invaded Southeast England, began the conquest of Wales, captured Angelsey where the Celtic Druids lived, built thriving towns and forts, set up Christianity
  • 7th century:the Saxons invaded eastern England until reached the borders of Wales
  • 9th century: Vikings attack, consistent attacks as well, yet most of Wales remained independent
  • 11th century: Welsh rebellion against English rule with Welsh kings as leaders in the movement
  • 16th century: during rise and fall of Protestantism,industrial boom, Wales grew richer on farming, cattle herding, coal mining, and wool industry
  • 18th century: hardshop for wales in terms of unemployment, also bombed during WWII
  • 20th century: new Welsh Assembly, population booming

Modern Demographics

  • Population Demographics: in 2020, the census reporterd a population of 3,169,586 people
    • 562,730 of them were aged 0 to 15
    • 1,938,266 of them were ages 16 to 64
    • 668,590 of them were aged 65 and over
  • Occupation demographics:
    • in 2019, 79.8% of males and 71.4% of females ages 16 to 64 were employed
    • in 2019, 17.4% of the population were economically inactive, meaning not working or actively seeking employment
  • Linguistic Demographics: in 2021, 29.1% of people aged three or older in Wales were able to speak Welsh, of this group 56% spoke the language daily
  • Ethnicity Demographics: in 2020, 94.8% of the population of Wales described their ethnic group as White
  • Disability Demographics: in 2020, 21.8% identified as disabled or differently abled
  • Religious Demogrpahics: in 2020, 47.9% of the population identified as Christian , 47.3% of the population stated they had no religion

Modern Accomplishments

  • Top Five Inventions by Welsh
    1. Edward Bowen: Part of a team of five people developing a radar to detect aircraft, built the transmitter which would bounce signals off of objects to determine their position
    2. Sir Pryce Pryce-Jones: Created the first Mail Order Business, ordered through catalogues and delivered through the Post Office and the new railway links, allowing people even in rural areas to get goods, set the foundation for online shopping
    3. Alfred Russel Wallace: One of the contributors alongside Darwin, shaped the Theory of Natural Selection and Evolution
    4. David Edward Hughes: Created the microphone, paving the way for recorded music

Sightseeing

Favorite Places Ive Visited so Far...
Cardiff Castle
Beautifully built and rich with history, one can spend many hours exploring the lively nooks and crannies of the Cardiff Castle, located conveniently in city centre. We spent multiple days here between classes. Since there are multiple sections of the castle to visit, we unconvered endless surprises! The main house includes the extensive library, stunning Moroccan room, and the glorious dining room with the friezes and stained glass windows. We also climbed to the top of fort in the center of the courtyard, where we got a breathtakingly unique perspective of the city. Finally, our favorite part was the tunnels underneath the castle wall which served as WWII bunkers!
Cardiff University
Every brochure and google search of Cardiff University displays the main building, which is stands strong and majestically across from the SU. However, I've chosen a picture of John Percival, the building where we have our weekly AMAZING cs course and other interesting Welsh courses. Though it is certainly not as beautiful as Hamilton, I have fallen in love with this campus and attending college in a city! I've loved the relaxing strolls to classes, getting lunch afterwards, the accesibility to city center, and not to mention the beautiful trees of campus!
Bristol
A couple weekends ago we took a train to the nearby city of Bristol. The train was quick and fairly easy to nagivate! I definitely hope to use the train system more often to explore more of the UK. In Bristol, the first thing we did was get food of course!!! As we made our way towards St. Nicholas Market for food, we passed by St Mary Redcliffe, a beautiful 12th century church. We also came across an abandoned 11th century church, which stood tucked away behind modern shops and buildings. A true shame to see history be neglected as such! After exploring the various shops filled with delectable goodies, we finally gorged ourselves on the food stalls from all the corners of the world! When we were satisfied we went to Bristol Cathedral, with parts of it dating back to before the 11th century. It blew our minds that such an old structure still stood so sturdy and elegant. Finally we ended our day by watching the "sunset" (in quotations because as the UK is known for, the sun is a myth) at the Clifton Suspension Bridge. Overall, a fun day trip! I loved that Bristol felt much more European with it's old Gothic structures.
